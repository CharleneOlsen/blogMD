I'm currently a student at a [**University of Chemistry and Technology in Prague**](https://www.vscht.cz/?jazyk=en) studying *Chemical engineering and bioengineering*. I am in my first year of getting a title of an Engineer. 

My path has not been always clear. I have for the longest time thought I would be doing something very different in my life, but here I am, and I'm very happy that I chose this path. 

## Why have I chosen this particular field to study?

When I was in the middle of choosing who I wanted to be, I was oscillating between many professions. I was initially interested in *particle physics* and/or *quantum physics*. Later, in my third year of gymnasium (or high school for non-Europeans) I was seriously thinking about political science or urbanism. The last year, it was right after COVID, so years 2021/2022, I landed on chemistry. And even though I have landed on the subject I still haven't figured out the specialisation. Fortunately, I was privileged enough to be able to choose anything my heart desired. 

After a long time of consideration I set my eyes on my beloved Alma mater and on *Nano and microtechnology in chemical engineering* for my bachelors and now full on *Chemical engineering and bioengineering* at a **University of Chemistry and Technology in Prague**. 

And I can tell you from the bottom of my heart, choosing this field was the best decision in my life. **Why?** Because it offers such a wide range of opportunities. I fulfilled my desire to study math, chemistry and physics. I code almost every day, I attend interesting lectures, I am now in a great laboratory where I research quite important topics. I can, and am, doing everything I have ever wanted. 

I'm not sure where my desire for being a researcher came from, but since I was little, I wanted to become a scientist, publish papers, give lectures and attend conferences, and in my ripe age of 23 I can say I did fulfil my dream. Well, almost. I haven't yet published a paper, but I am working on it. 

## What is so special about chemical engineering?

Later on, I want to dedicate a whole note for this, but for now shorter explanation will do. 

Chemical engineering, as any engineering really, is a perfect choice for anybody who wants to combine multiple knowledge pools into one. As I said earlier, chemical engineering is not just chemistry, it is at large also math and physics with a pinch of biology (if you want). But these sciences are not all of it. I would argue you need to understand economy, laws or regulations, also a bit of sociology and other topics. 

Because chemical engineering is not really just about chemistry and how to create something. Main job of a chemical engineer is to take what is already created, like some kind of process, and scale it up. And you may think, what of it? You can just run the same reaction but in a bigger volume. But that's not really true for most processes. It is very often, that reaction runs beautifully in a smaller reactor because you can regulate temperature more effectively, no heat spots are created, so everything runs smoothly and uniformly. But when scaling up, there is just so much that can go wrong. Maybe you find out, that one mixer is not enough to mix bigger volume, maybe you haven't accounted for rise or decrease of viscosity, or maybe a creation of side products. There is just quite a lot that can happen.

But that is what is so amazing. 

I remember we had an excursion to a factory that makes paper (yes, even here you need a chemical engineer) and our tour guide talked about how his typical day looks like. And he said, that the work of a chemical engineer is a lot like a detective work. He said that he comes back to work in the morning, and he is briefed on what went wrong at night, and it is his job to find out who or what messed things up. 

This is just few examples of how this job can look like. But to tell the truth, and as I said before, chemical engineers have a huge range in what they can do, because everybody needs an engineer that would figure out how to start, or continue production, or how to create new materials and so on.

I want to be a researcher, well I already am, but I want to continue it this path. I will, if destiny will help me, pursue a PhD, and we will see from there.
