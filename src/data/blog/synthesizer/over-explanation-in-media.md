You know what pisses me off in today's TV shows, movies and such? **Over-explanation**. I feel as if these media think I'm a kiddo when watching. Why do they have to just explain everything? Give me Chekhov gun, give me exposition, give me mystery. I don't want to hear the full story, I want to see and feel it. 

I've been watching a bit of new *Avatar: The Last Airbender* in Netflix adaptation. And oh boy, it's kind of painful. It looks gorgeous, but I can say that about pretty much any movies or series nowadays. The look is not the issue, but the content is. 

## Possibilities of movies and series as a medium
Movies and series are a medium that offers a huge range of possibilities to convey a meaning and story to a viewer. In their toolbox they have sound and image. This tickles two senses, seeing and hearing, and through these two senses we process and understand the majority of signals coming from this world. Our brains were built to find the deeper meaning, especially behind ones emotions and facial expressions. I would say, that movies and series are the easiest media to understand, I would say its on the same level as theater and games. 

Due to this range of possibilities, movies and series have a huge opportunity to convey any story the director and screenwriter desire. We have romance, science fiction, documentaries, and so much more. Furthermore, you can even see how this range creates different art styles. You can see it in how different directors set their stages, how they create the frame, what colours they use. For example *Denis Villeneuve* can create these huge spaces that feel so empty though so familiar, see Figure 1. So if skilled enough, or having a desire and creativity to create a story, these media can offer you a platform to do so.

## Reasons behind over-explanation
Knowing about all these possibilities, why are they choosing the over-explanation as a way to set up and progress the story? Well I know why, but I hate that reason. It is again that damn phone. Because the reason is that we have fried their brains with phones. We are soooo easily bored, and we can't stay bored, because well we are bored, and thus we need to do something with it. And what we do? What we do when standing in a line? Or when we have a spare 5 seconds? We reach for the phone, because we are addicted. 

And well, directors and screenwriters are addicted as well. So both parties understand that they from time to time, a very short period of time, need to get their fix. And this is the reason behind the rise of over-explanation. Because they need to keep viewers engaged and need to account for viewers not paying attention all the time. So from movies and series we got a YouTube video, which is basically a podcast with supplementary visual content.  

## Why is it bad?
Directors use this reason as a way out, because if people are not watching all the time, you don't have to spend a lot of time creating a profound picture, you only need a visual anchor. Viewers won't pay attention to details. No need for expensive garments to immerse viewer in the story, no need to create huge sets, or really anything. Also, no need for a well written script. No need to come up with allegories, with profound twists. The machine can be oiled up to pump out the most average product. It's much cheaper, takes less time and in the end will amount to a same number of views, which is the only metric that production companies are interested in (and money, of course money).

Over-explanation in the end is the byproduct of over simplification of everything. Our speech is simplifying, we use less sophisticated language, everything needs to be breakdown. Of course, sometimes it's necessary to convey a difficult subject to a pupil, but not everything. 

I truly feel stupid when I watch some of the movies nowadays, I feel as if they have to do it all because I have a mind of a child. I love to explore difficult topics, but in the end I don't get even a chance to get the satisfaction of solving some mystery. 

At the beginning of the MARVEL cinematic universe, I loved how they explored for example Tony's depression and PTSD or Cap's emotional trauma due to time jump. In *Age of Ultron*, I loved how they explored the whole concept of AI and the universal surveillance of the planet and any extraterrestrial threats. But today it is all the same. Last few movies from MARVEL felt emotionless, so uneventful. They pump so much fan service into movies to be fun and appealing for all that in the end it's not interesting. Same thing happened to me with the ninth movies in the Star Wars universe *The Rise of Skywalker*. Same issue. 

I'm not saying that all is bad. If I linger in the Star Wars universe, I LOVE *Andor*. Oh my goodness. It's just so good. It explores so many interesting topics. There is whole manifesto. I just love it from the bottom of my heart. *Andor* is an example of modern series that is incredibly well written.

## Conclusion?
What's the way out here? To be real, I don't know. I feel as if corporations killed the sparkle of so many universes in favour of profit. And I hate it. I feel sad for the crew that made it, because they have done an incredible job. 

I hope that in the future we will see a lot of projects made by people for people. I really hope so.
