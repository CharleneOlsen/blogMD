I just found out, that there are such amazing things as a blog or web carnivals, and gosh I love the idea. So, I looked around on the topics available, and I settled on my first carnival [Bear Blog](https://grizzlygazette.bearblog.dev/carnival/) with the topic of *An album that got you through a hard time* hosted by [Evie](https://ev-fae.quest/slashes/now/).

I think this topic is exactly what I needed. 

I listen to music almost all the time, some may say that it's unhealthy, and they are most probably right, but oh well. And so music plays a huge role in my life. I listen to quite a lot of different artists and in different genres. I like indie rock, indie folk, folklore as genre of itself, rock, metal, classical music and so on. I kind of pride myself in knowing a lot of artists and their music. Among my favourite artists are *AURORA* (now also *TOMORA*), *Florence and The Machine*, *Faun*, *Daughter*, *Low Roar*, *Joji*, *Heilung* and many many more.

But I would like to talk about an album that helped me. And I won't probably fit in the correct term of an album, because this album was not made only by one artist, but it is rather a playlist of different songs. I'm talking here about a soundtrack, album or a playlist from the first game of *Life Is Strange*. I wrote already about Life Is Strange [here](https://alchemistsnotes.com/notes/synthesizer/games-and-their-influence/) and its effect on me, but I haven't fully explored its music and what it meant to me.  

Life Is Strange as a game had a huge influence on the way I view friendships, relationships, time travel and just self exploration and music in huge sense supported this notion. 

## Track list
There are 14 licensed songs and 8 score tracks by Jonathan Morali. I'm going to mainly reflect on those 14 tracks, because those were one that touched my soul very deeply.

This track list was there when I needed it. When people broke my heart, when I had issue with my family, when school sucked hard. What is so great about it is that there are songs for any occasion. For happy and sad times, for life and death, for love and break up. 

### 1. Syd Matters - To All of You
There is something so sweet, innocent and warm in this track list at the beginning. The song is just a balm on a soul. This is the song plays when Max is in school in the first episode. You see how she is shy and wears her earphones whilst in the corridor. This is truly me all the time. 

### 2. José González - Crosses
Oh Crosses. Oh my, if I had to recommend you only one song from this track list, is this one. I have so many memories connected to this song. But first, the artist. José González is an artistic genius, I truly believe that. He is so virtuous with his guitar and his voice just transports you somewhere else. In the game, Max plays this song in the beginning of the second episode. 

For me, the song Crosses is associated with one guy, with whom I didn't have good timing. He was first to show his affection towards me, but at that time, I wasn't really ready. And later on I was the one showing him affection, but he was already somewhere else. For me, this song evokes such nostalgic emotions. I listened to it a lot when I was younger while walking through narrow streets of Prague. I was always reminiscing about what could have been, what never happened if I chose different path, and it always brought me horrible pain. It still does. 

> People staring they know you've been broken
> Repeatedly reminded by the looks on their faces
> Ignore them tonight and you'll be alright

I was never really lucky in my love life, mainly because I had never really learnt to love myself, because I do believe that in order to love someone you need to learn to love yourself. My whole life I felt very broken, that there is truly something bad about me, something broken inside myself. I still feel this way, so unfortunately I can't give any sufficient way to get out of this feeling. 

### 5. Alt-J - Something Good
This was my first song I have ever heard by Alt-J and my goodness it stuck with me for life. The whole repertoire of Alt-J is amazing, their sound is so profound and so interesting, that is what stuck with me the most. 

> Oh, something good tonight
> Will make me forget about you for now

Even though the song is about Matadors, the song for me is really about trying to forget. 

### 11. Foals - Spanish Sahara
Well well well. 

*SPOILERS FOR THE ENDING OF LIFE IS STRANGE!!*

This song plays after you make your final decision, to sacrifice Chloe your best friend you spend the whole game with, or you sacrifice the Arcadia Bay, city filled with amazing and horrible people. 

Foals plays after you sacrifice Chloe, and my, this song sets the scene. It's very heavy song and heavy scene where you watch how Chloe gets killed, and you just sit there and wait for everything to unravel. It creates a horrible pit in your stomach, because even though you did a good thing by saving hundreds of lives, game makes you truly feel the burden of your choice. 

I had to suffer through death of my friend that had killed themselves. It was truly awful year then, and I miss them dearly. They were funny, just amazing human being and this song reminds me of them every day. 

> Forget the horror here
> Leave it all down here 
> It's future rust and it's future dust

These lyrics lay very heavy on my chest every time I hear them. 

## Legacy
From all these artists few of them stuck with me my whole life. Notably, *José González*, *Bright Eyes*, *Foals* but mainly *Alt-J*. 

After I played the game I listened to Alt-J all the time, I even wanted to go to their concert, but shortly after I found out about them, they stopped touring, so it was a huge bummer. 

But this playlist was main building block of my taste in music. Because of Life Is Strange I fell in love with indie rock as a genre.
