In my spare time I have decided to dive into a new hot thing in AI community called [Karpathy LLM wiki](https://gist.github.com/karpathy/442a6bf555914893e9891c11519de94f). I am a researcher, so I thought it might be useful for me, because this thing presents itself as a way to handle a big chunk of files you might have stored on your computer or elsewhere. Files can range from research papers, YouTube videos or just web clips all wrapped in easy to use UI of Obsidian vault. 

The LLM wiki, if you are not familiar, is wiki that is build from your raw sources. It is build with the use of LLM (Large Language Model) and it ingests the sources to create a notes based on the fed materials. For UI people (and me) often use Obsidian, which is an app to handle and create file in markdown (.md) file type which then can be used to communicated with LLM. 

On paper, it sounds amazing. I just upload the data, which as a scientist I have way too much, and it categorises it, sorts it and creates notes on important things found in sources. Notes are created automatically or by your request. 

Well, I have built one on my computer using [this guide](https://youtu.be/QbjAQFJJyt0). But then, what of it? I can feed it more data, but I found it very useless in this state and let me explain why.

## My issue with Karpathy's idea
>"You never (or rarely) write the wiki yourself — the LLM writes and maintains all of it. You're in charge of sourcing, exploration, and asking the right questions. The LLM does all the grunt work — the summarizing, cross-referencing, filing, and bookkeeping that makes a knowledge base actually useful over time."

I agree that sorting and going thorough sources is annoying. BUT. And I found that nobody really addresses this (I found only Reddit treads) that sorting and going through the data, papers, PDF files is how you learn. Yes, having notes in a wiki is nice, but learning, in my case and I would say most of the researchers, is happening while YOU do "the grunt work".

Because **learning is suffering.** Learning has to feel uncomfortable, because our brains don't want to do it. But you learn most when you are re-reading that paragraph in the research paper for the twentieth time. You are learning when you highlight, when you rewrite sentences in your own words, because ingesting and summarising, that AI does here for you, is when you chew up the source to ingest and categorise it in your brain. 

So you may ask, Charli, you are the person that is very against AI, why even trying to create this in the first place? Well, this question has two answers. One, real one, is that I was kind of bored during this exam season, and my brain needed to switch things up to do something else just not endlessly deriving equations, which I love, but you can't love it 24/7. Second, there is a saying "curiosity killed the cat". I just wanted to try the new shiny fancy thing and tick of some kind of box in my head. 

## So what now?
I'm still debating what is the true purpose of this wiki for me. Because now I think, the main goal is to treat it as a NotebookLM, which In my opinion is the only true useful AI tool. And it was the main idea at the beginning. Because NotebookLM is great at chatting with provided sources. It prevents the hallucinations of AI, when it comes up with for example non-existing research paper, or comes to untrue conclusions. 

But for now, I can't justify it. It just seems as a way to feel more productive without actually doing the work. Because you see how the graph grows, and might think that you are learning something. But in reality, you are just coping stuff into AI without really knowing what you are coping. You might read the notes created by the AI, but you won't really truly fully understand what is written there without actually diving very deep into the topic.

If you want to learn something, just read. It saves so much time. You don't have to download anything, you are just using the ability that nature and natural selection worked on for about 4 billion years. It is the best ability we have, reading and comprehending what we are reading. And we are improving ourselves truly only through that. Nothing will truly expedite that. Because it is easier to create wiki than actually spend time learning. 

And reading and understanding is a skill which means we can develop it and be better at it. Invest your time in developing yourself, instead of wasting your precious time on this project. 

## How would I use it?
But okay, I digress. Let's imagine, we have created this wiki. How can we implement in our daily work? Or how I would imagine the best scenario of usage. 

I think the best way of using this system, is to take the raw source and ingest it by yourself. Meaning you would take notes, highlight everything you found useful, write your own thought and just then you would copy all of that with the raw source to LLM wiki for further categorisation and sorting by the LLM. 

That would mean that you went through it, comprehended what is written there and just then you can communicate with this source. Because how can you ask questions without at least some basic understanding of the subject. 

By the way, for highlighting I would recommend Zotero, where you can view your PDF file, highlight and use Zotero plugin in Obsidian to export not only PDF and citation but also highlights you made. I find it very useful and fitting the overall workflow. And it is actually useful tool for any researcher or/and self-learner.

>**But in the end, the best and most productive system is one you will actively use and maintain, because without that it is just a waste of time.**
