It is easy to write a Chemical reaction, all these fancy numbers and elements, but it is much harder to think of how to recreate such reaction in the lab, let alone in big production scale.

Every reaction is different, every production is different.

This is actually where mostly all innovation die. It all due to the scalability of the process. If you can't figure out the to scale your production, your product is not viable and will likely not survive.


--
I'm currently a student in a University of Chemistry and Technology in Prague studying Chemical engineering and bioengineering. I am in my first year of getting a Masters diploma or a title of an Engineer. 

Why have I chosen this particular field to study?

When I was in mids of choosing who I wanted to be I was oscillating between many professions. I was initially interested in Particle physics and/or Quantum Physics. Later, in a third year of middle school I was seriously thinking about politology and urbanism. The last year, it was right after covid, so years 2021/2022 I landed on chemistry. There I was still uncertain what exactly I wanted to study. Fortunately, I was privileged enough to be able to choose anything my heart desired. And so, I landed on my beloved alma mater and on Nano and microtechnology in chemical engineering for my bachelors and now full on chemical engineering and bioengineering. 

And I can tell you from the bottom of my heart, choosing this field was the best decision in my life. Why? Because it offers such a wide range of opportunities. I fullfiled my desire to study math, chemistry and physics. I code almost every day, I attend interesting lectures, I am now in a great laboratory where I research quite important topics. I can, and am, doing every I want. 

When I was little, I wanted to become a scientic, publish papers, give lectures and attend cofferences, and in my ripe age of 23 I can say I did fulfill my dream. Well, almost. I haven't yet published a paper, but I am working on it.
